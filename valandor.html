The Emperor's Doubt

Valandor has ruled for 1,500 years. He's seen everything. And lately, he's begun to wonder if the Empire can survive what's coming.

His private fears:

  • The drow genocide created enemies who will never forgive
  • The Dragon War wasn't entirely the drow's fault (see below)
  • Thaelen's defection exposed a truth: even the best can choose exile over Empire
  • The world is changing faster than elves can adapt

What he does: Nothing visible. He watches. He thinks. He hasn't shared these doubts with anyoneâ€”not even Isileth, whom he trusts most.

The Dragon War Truth

The official history: Drow tortured dragons, breaking sacred bonds. The Empire righteously exterminated them.

The hidden truth (known only to Valandor, Velindor Shadowmere, and the deepest archives):

  • The war began over territory, not dragons
  • Both sides committed atrocities
  • The dragon-torture was real but was retaliation, not unprovoked
  • Elves struck first
  • The genocide was Valandor's rage, not justice

Why it matters: If this became public, the Empire's moral foundation crumbles. 400 years of "righteous" killing become just... killing.

The Succession Question

Valandor is immortal but not invulnerable. If he dies:

  • No designated heir
  • Four Pillars would struggle for control
  • Civil war possible (first in 1,500 years)
  • The Emperor refuses to discuss it

Why: Valandor believes naming an heir would invite assassination attempts. He's probably right. But his silence creates its own danger.

Why He Let Thaelen Go

Thaelen discovered the Dragon War truth. Velindor tried to stop him. Valandor could have had Thaelen killed.

He didn't. He let the last dragon rider leave rather than risk the truth spreading. In a way, Thaelen's defection was Valandor's mercyâ€”and his shame.
