400 Years Ago - 35 Days That Changed Everything Forever

Timeline: 400 years ago
Duration: 35 days (devastating despite brevity)
Cause: Drow experimentation on dragons, mistakenly attributed to elves
Result: Dragon-bonding tradition ended, dragons retreated to northwestern mountains
Sole Survivor with Bond: Galdor Ruincalen and Thorongil
True Culprits: The Drow
Consequence: 400 years of drow genocide by Elven Empire

The Dragon-Elf Bond (Before the War)

The Symbiotic Relationship

For millennia, elves and dragons shared a unique connection in the northwestern mountains.

The Bond:

  • Natural and spontaneous: Young dragons would form connections with elves
  • Like falling in love: Came with deep care, loyalty, and understanding
  • Mysterious: No one understood the basis of the connection
  • Mutual choice: Both dragon and elf had to accept the bond
  • Unbreakable: Once formed, the bond lasted for life

Dragon Riders:

  • Not chosen or trained first - the bond came first, training followed
  • Discovered after bonding - you became a rider when a dragon chose you
  • Trained as Dragon Knights - elite warriors, protectors of the bond
  • Sacred duty - honored position in elven society

Dragon Society:

Location: Dragons populated the mountains to the northwest of the Elven Empire

  • Bonded dragons: Chose to connect with elves, lived in harmony
  • Elder dragons: Never approved of the elf-dragon connection but didn't interfere
  • Neutral stance: Tolerated the bonds as individual choice, not species policy

Important: Dragons were free. They chose the bonds. They chose to partner with elves.

The Drow's Envy

Why Drow Wanted the Bond

The drow never formed natural bonds with dragons.

  • No dragon ever chose a drow
  • Reasons unknown (racism? Cultural difference? Something else?)
  • This exclusion bred resentment and obsession

The Dark Experiments

Unable to earn a dragon's love, the drow tried to force it:

  • Capturing dragons: Kidnapping young and vulnerable dragons
  • Magical experimentation: Attempting to create artificial bonds through spells
  • Torture and conditioning: Breaking dragons' wills to force obedience
  • Arcane research: Dark rituals to mimic the natural connection

Location: Hidden drow cities, far from elven observation
Duration: Unknown how long this went on before discovery

The 35-Day War

The Attack (Day 1)

The elder dragons discovered the truth: Drow were capturing and torturing dragons.

Critical Mistake

The elder dragons thought the elves were responsible.

Reasoning:

  • Dragon riders were elves
  • Captured dragons had been taken from elven territory
  • Drow remained hidden, their cities secret
  • No one knew drow were even capable of such magic

The elder dragons' rage: They had tolerated the bonding, but forcing dragons to serve was unforgivable.

The Surprise Attack

The elder dragons launched a devastating assault on the Elven Empire:

  • No warning: Complete surprise attack
  • Overwhelming force: Dragons who had never fought elves, now did
  • Bonded dragons faced impossible choice: Fight their species or their riders

The Impossible War (35 Days)

For the bonded dragons:

  • Torn between species and chosen family
  • Attacked by their own kind for "serving" elves
  • Defending riders meant fighting other dragons
  • Many chose to break bonds and flee rather than fight

For the elves:

  • Couldn't understand why dragons attacked
  • Their greatest allies became their greatest enemies
  • Dragon Knights had to fight dragons
  • Devastating casualties despite elite training

The Outcome:

  • Most bonded dragons: Either killed fighting alongside elves, or broke bonds and fled
  • Elder dragons: Furious, unyielding, determined to punish
  • Elven Empire: Desperately trying to understand what they'd done wrong

Galdor Ruincalen - The Last Bond

The Only Survivor

Galdor Ruincalen and his dragon Thorongil were the only bonded pair to survive.

Why they survived:

  • Galdor never killed a dragon: Fought defensively only
  • Beat them into retreat: Powerful enough to win without killing
  • His dragon's loyalty: Absolute, unshakeable, even against his own kind
  • Mutual protection: Each protected the other perfectly

To the dragons:

  • Galdor's dragon is a traitor - sided with elves over dragons
  • Cannot be killed - not while bonded to Galdor
  • Living reminder of the war and broken trust

To the elves:

  • Last dragon rider - symbol of what was lost
  • Most valuable warrior - irreplaceable after the war
  • Living connection to the dragon-bonding era

The Truth Revealed

Years of Investigation

It took years for the elves to discover the truth:

  • Drow cities were hidden
  • Surviving dragons wouldn't speak to elves
  • Evidence had to be painstakingly gathered
  • Pengolodh and the Council of Memory investigated thoroughly

The Discovery

The elves finally learned:

  • DROW had been capturing and experimenting on dragons
  • DROW had caused the war through their actions
  • DRAGONS had attacked the wrong target
  • EVERYTHING was the drow's fault

The Elven Response

The drow would pay.

Emperor C√≠rdan Golvenion decreed the harshest punishment in elven history:

  • Kill on sight: All drow, everywhere, no exceptions
  • No investigation: Being drow is sufficient crime
  • No trials: The species is guilty
  • Genocidal policy: Lasted 400 years and continues today

Reasoning (official):

  • They caused the loss of the dragon bonds
  • They tortured dragons
  • They destroyed millennia of harmony
  • They must be eradicated

Reasoning (emotional):

  • Rage at being betrayed
  • Grief over lost bonds
  • Shame at fighting dragons
  • Someone must pay for the loss

Current State (400 Years Later)

The Dragons

Location: Northwestern mountains, far from elves

  • No contact: Dragons want nothing to do with elves
  • No new bonds: The natural bonding never returned
  • Deep mistrust: Still believe elves were responsible? Or know but don't forgive?
  • Living their own lives: Separate from all humanoid races

Thorongil: Considered traitor by other dragons. Cannot return to dragon society. Lives openly atop Veraheim as guardian. Forever bonded to Galdor.

The Elven Empire

  • Dragon Knights: Now just elite warriors, no dragons
  • Training persists but the bond is lost
  • Ecthelion maintains the order
  • They know they're shadows of what they were

The drow: Drow Persecution continues - 400 years of genocide. Every drow killed on sight in Empire. Human Kingdom won't publicly deal with drow to avoid angering elves.

The Drow

  • Living in hiding: Secret cities, hidden from elven eyes
  • Secret trade networks
  • Cannot exist openly anywhere
  • Veraheim is their only sanctuary (15% of population)

The Great Tragedy

No one won.
  • Dragons: Lost many of their kind, lost trust in elves, isolated
  • Elves: Lost the sacred bonds forever, lost many riders
  • Drow: Face genocide for 400 years
  • Galdor & Thorongil: Only survivors, alone with their bond
