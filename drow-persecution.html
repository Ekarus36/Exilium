üåë Drow Persecution

400 Years of Genocide - The Crime Was Centuries Ago, The Punishment Continues

Imperial Decree (400 Years Running):
"All drow are to be killed on sight. No exceptions. No trials. The species is guilty."
- Emperor C√≠rdan Golvenion

Duration: 400 years
Policy: Systematic genocide
Status: Ongoing, actively enforced

The Original Crime (400+ Years Ago)

What the Drow Did:

  • Captured dragons in hidden operations
  • Experimented on them with dark magic
  • Attempted to force dragon bonds artificially
  • Tortured dragons to break their will
  • Conducted these experiments in secret for unknown duration

Why They Did It:

  • Envied elves' natural dragon bonds
  • No dragon ever chose a drow naturally
  • Wanted the power and prestige of dragon riders
  • Tried to force what they couldn't earn

The Consequence:

  • Elder dragons discovered the experiments
  • Dragons blamed elves (riders are elves)
  • Launched the Dragon War (35 days of devastation)
  • All dragon-elf bonds destroyed except one
  • Dragons retreated, no contact for 400 years

The Punishment - 400 Years of Genocide

Emperor C√≠rdan's Decree:

"The drow destroyed our sacred bonds with the dragons. They tortured and experimented on our kin. For this crime, the entire species must be eradicated. Kill all drow on sight. No trials. No exceptions. This is my decree."

The Policy:

  • Kill on sight: Any drow, anywhere in Empire
  • No investigation: Being drow is sufficient crime
  • No trials: The species is guilty
  • No mercy: Children, elderly, everyone
  • Bounties: Rewarded for killing drow
  • 400 years: Policy never rescinded

The Logic:

  • They caused loss of dragon bonds
  • They tortured dragons
  • They destroyed millennia of harmony
  • All drow benefit from the crime (in elven view)
  • Therefore all must pay

The Reality - 400 Years Later

Who Dies Today:

  • Drow born 300 years after the crime
  • Drow who never knew those responsible
  • Drow children who've done nothing
  • Drow who condemn what their ancestors did
  • Every drow, regardless of individual guilt

The crime was 400 years ago. The criminals are long dead. The genocide continues.

How Drow Survive:

  • Hidden Cities: Secret settlements, constantly moving
  • Underground Networks: Complex escape routes
  • False Identities: Disguise magic when possible
  • Veraheim: Only safe sanctuary (15% of population)
  • Human Kingdom: Tolerated but not safe (lords won't protect them)

Population Impact:

  • Once numerous, now rare
  • Exact numbers unknown (hiding)
  • Most live in Veraheim or hidden cities
  • Birth rate cannot keep up with deaths
  • Species may not survive another 400 years

Veraheim - The Only Sanctuary

Safe Haven

Drow Population in Veraheim: ~525 (15% of city)

Why It's Safe:

  • All races equal policy (enforced absolutely)
  • Galdor and Thorongil protect everyone
  • Vera will not tolerate racism
  • Empire cannot attack (economic dependence)
  • Dragon on peak deters aggression

What It Means:

  • First time in 400 years drow live openly
  • Can walk streets without fear
  • Children grow up without hiding
  • Proof that another way is possible

Mirelen Dawnwhisper (3rd Pillar, Drow, 180 years): Lives in Veraheim, commutes to Tirion under special protection. Only drow in Empire proper. Bridge between worlds.

The Moral Questions

The Elven Position:

  • "They destroyed the dragon bonds"
  • "They tortured our kin"
  • "The crime was unforgivable"
  • "All drow benefit from the original crime"
  • "Justice demands this"

The Counter-Arguments:

  • The criminals are dead (400 years ago)
  • Modern drow had nothing to do with it
  • Collective punishment is genocide
  • Children are innocent
  • When does justice become vengeance?
  • 400 years is enough

Veraheim's Position:

"The crime was real. The loss was devastating. But modern drow are not responsible for what their ancestors did 400 years ago. Genocide is never justice."

- Vera Wyrdweaver

Can It End?

The Challenge:

  • C√≠rdan decreed it personally (won't back down)
  • 400 years of momentum (cultural inertia)
  • Dragon bonds still lost (wound unhealed)
  • Many elves lost family in Dragon War
  • Collective trauma passed down generations

What Would It Take:

  • C√≠rdan changing his mind (unlikely after 1,500 years)
  • Or C√≠rdan's death (he's immortal)
  • Dragon reconciliation (might ease pain)
  • Drow reparations (what could possibly suffice?)
  • Time (how much? another 400 years?)

The Hope:

Veraheim proves integration works. 525 drow live openly, contribute, thrive. When elves visit Veraheim, they see drow as people, not abstract "species-guilty." Slowly, very slowly, minds might change.

But not in C√≠rdan's lifetime. And he's immortal.

Campaign Implications

For Drow Players:

  • Cannot safely enter Elven Empire
  • Hunted if discovered
  • Veraheim is sanctuary
  • Constant tension and fear
  • Rich roleplaying opportunity

For DMs:

  • Moral complexity (no easy answers)
  • Historical trauma vs. present innocence
  • Can players change centuries of policy?
  • Or must they work within unjust system?

Story Hooks:

  • Help drow refugees reach Veraheim
  • Investigate hidden drow cities
  • Negotiate with Empire on behalf of drow
  • Discover evidence that could change C√≠rdan's mind
  • Protect drow from Imperial hunters

Drow Persecution: 400 years of genocide for crimes committed by those long dead. When does justice become vengeance? When does punishment become evil?
