üí´ Vera Wyrdweaver

The Lady of Veraheim - Most Powerful Sorcerer in Exilium

Race: Human
Age: 100 years old (appears ~30 due to magical aging)
Class: Sorcerer (innate magic, not studied)
Title: Co-Founder and Co-Ruler of Veraheim, "The Lady," "The Mountain Splitter"
Status: Most powerful sorcerer in Exilium by far

Appearance

  • Appears in her early 30s despite being 100 years old
  • Her immense magical power slows her aging (not intentional)
  • Hair: Dark brown to black, shoulder-length, naturally wavy
  • Eyes: Striking green eyes (hint of her magical power)
  • Height: Average to slightly tall (~5'7"-5'9")
  • Build: Slender but carries herself with quiet strength
  • Distinctive: Eyes glow faint green when using magic
  • Clothing: Rich teal/emerald robes with gold embroidery
  • Jewelry: Multiple layered necklaces (magical foci)

Personality

Core Character: Pragmatic Realist with Tempered Idealism

80 Years Ago: Young idealist who believed love and power could change the world
Now: Experienced leader who knows idealism has limits, but refuses to abandon it entirely

Key Traits:

  • Pragmatic: Makes hard choices because someone has to
  • Self-Aware: Constantly questions herself to avoid corruption
  • Decisive: 80 years of leadership means decisions come as second nature
  • Burdened: Carries the weight of those she cannot save
  • Principled: Won't compromise core values despite pragmatism
  • Honest: Brutal honesty about limitations (including her own)

Magical Power

Unmatched Sorcerer

To Defeat Her Magically: Would require the top 10 trained wizards in the realm, working as a coordinated team. Even then, victory is not guaranteed.

Why She's Unmatched:

  • Sorcerer, not wizard - magic is innate, part of her being
  • 100 years of practice = mastery through experience
  • Instinctive casting - magic is second nature, no thought needed
  • Reflexes honed - decisions and reactions happen automatically
  • No decline - power hasn't weakened with age

Mountain-Splitting Power:

80 years ago, split a mountain range in rage/desperation. Could do it again if necessary, though it would require recovery time. That level of power is weapon of last resort.

Veraheim's Magic Infrastructure:

All city magic runs on Vera's power:

  • The Three Lifts: Animated objects under her command
  • Can command them remotely from anywhere in city
  • Can disable all three simultaneously if threatened
  • If she's incapacitated, lifts stop (safety measure)

Daily Life

Morning (Dawn to Mid-Morning):

  • Wakes at dawn naturally (100 years of habit)
  • Private breakfast with Galdor (quiet time together)
  • Morning meditation/magic attunement (maintains city enchantments, 30 min)
  • Brief review of overnight reports

Mid-Morning to Afternoon (Working Hours):

  • Public audiences (twice weekly) - citizens petition for help, dispute resolution
  • Administrative work (daily) - budget approvals, policy decisions, resource allocation
  • City inspections (weekly) - walks through districts, checks infrastructure
  • Meetings with craft guilds and merchant councils

Evening (Personal Time):

  • Private dinner with Galdor (their sacred time)
  • Courtesy visits and informal meetings
  • Evening conversations with Thorongil (3-4x per week)
  • Climbs to his peak for philosophical discussions and moral check-ins

The Floating Docks - Her Moral Burden

Her View: "We Can't Save Everyone"

"Where do we draw the line? If we help the Floats, they'll grow. More will come. Do we expand to 4,000? 5,000? 10,000? At what point does Veraheim collapse trying to save everyone?"

Her Logic:

  • Veraheim can support 3,500 people sustainably
  • Expanding means lower quality of life for current residents
  • Resources are finite (food, housing, guards, magic)
  • The Floats are overflow valve preventing worse alternatives

Her Guilt:

Sees the Floats from her castle every single day. Knows children are drowning, people starving. This eats at her, but she won't change course.

The Halfling Girl Story:

Five years ago, an 8-year-old halfling girl from the Floats approached her: "Lady Vera, why can't my family live here? We'd work hard."

The girl explained they spent everything on medicine for her baby brother.

Vera gave the family gold anonymously (enough for two months), but didn't change the policy.

This haunts her more than anything. She knows she drew an arbitrary line. She knows she's a hypocrite. She never told anyone except Thorongil.

Preventing Corruption - Her Safeguards

"Power Corrupts, and I Have Enormous Power"

1. Conversations with Thorongil (The Dragon Counsel):

  • 400+ years of experience, not human, loves her
  • Challenges her reasoning, points out blind spots
  • Reminds her of original ideals
  • Tells her when she's wrong (and she listens)

2. Trusted Moral Advisors (The Inner Circle):

  • Galdor Ruincalen - Partner, 400 years perspective
  • Mirelen Dawnwhisper - Drow elder (180), survived genocide 160 years
  • Borin Ironfoot - Dwarf master smith, working-class perspective
  • Marigold Tealeaf - Halfling grandmother from the Floats (secret advisor)
  • Kellan Ashvale - Retired guard captain, practical ethics

3. Self-Imposed Limits:

  • Never use full power unless city threatened
  • Never harm someone weaker without cause
  • Always question her own motives
  • Listen to those who disagree
  • Admit when wrong (publicly if necessary)

Relationship with Galdor

80 Years Together - Still Deeply in Love

  • Still passionate after 80 years (not just comfortable)
  • The fire has matured but hasn't dimmed
  • Run the city together as true partners
  • Public: Professional, united front, respectful
  • Private: Warm, affectionate, playful
  • Arguments are productive (she: "Act now", he: "Think it through")
  • Never go to bed angry (their 80-year rule)

Division of Responsibilities:

  • Vera: City magic, infrastructure, economy, policy
  • Galdor: Military, security, guard command, external threats
  • Together: Major decisions, diplomacy, long-term planning

Relationship with Thorongil

The Dragon She Learned to Love

The Beginning (80 years ago): Thorongil was wary, protective of Galdor. She was the human intruder into their sacred dragon-elf bond.

The Slow Build:

  • First decade: Tolerated her for Galdor's sake
  • Second decade: Respect grew as he saw her character
  • Third decade: True affection developed
  • After 30 years: Thorongil opened telepathic link to her

Now (After 80 Years):

  • She rides him (privilege that took 30 years to earn)
  • They have philosophical discussions (evening counsels)
  • He is her moral compass and confessor
  • She trusts him completely
  • Strategic patrols, emergency flights, personal flights for clarity

Her Voice & Manner

Voice/Tone - Warm but Firm:

  • Default: Warm, approachable, genuinely kind
  • When needed: Firm, commanding, brooks no argument
  • Speaks clearly and directly (no flowery language)
  • Listens more than she speaks
  • Remembers names and details

Triggers for Anger (rare but intense):

  1. Substantial threats to Veraheim - Eyes glow green, voice goes deadly quiet
  2. Accusations of corruption - Takes personally, defends fiercely
  3. Harm to Galdor or Thorongil - Loses all restraint, most dangerous

Her Greatest Fears

  1. Losing Galdor - 80 years with him, couldn't bear to continue alone
  2. Becoming a tyrant - The power is there, one bad day could break her restraint
  3. Veraheim failing - Everything she built crumbling, proving cynics right
  4. Being right about the Floats - What if the pragmatism is correct? That's almost worse

All four fears haunt her in different ways. The Galdor fear keeps her human. The tyrant fear keeps her moral. The Veraheim fear keeps her working. The Floats fear keeps her awake at night.

Quotes

"I split a mountain once. It nearly killed me. Power without restraint is just destruction."
"Where do we draw the line? Tell me. Because wherever I draw it, someone will be on the wrong side."
"I am responsible for 3,500 lives. Not 4,000. Not 500. The 3,500 inside these walls. That is where I draw my line."
"Every night I ask Thorongil: Am I losing my way? Every night I fear his answer."
"I still believe another way is possible. I just learned it has limits. We all have limits."
