Ten Ancient Cities of the Elven Empire

Overview

The Elven Empire consists of ten enclaves spread across the western lands. Each is an ancient city built over centuries, integrated with nature, and home to thousands of elves. Together they house ~150,000 elves total.

Total Population: ~150,000 elves across all enclaves
Government: Each enclave governed by local council, reports to Emperor
Connection: Well-maintained roads between enclaves, 3-7 days travel

The Ten Enclaves

1. Tirion - The Capital

Population: ~50,000 elves
Location: Central western lands
Significance: Imperial capital, Emperor's palace, Four Pillars headquarters

Largest and most important enclave. Center of government, culture, and power. Ancient white towers rise above ancient forest. Emperor C√≠rdan's palace dominates skyline.

2. Mithlond - The Grey Havens

Population: ~15,000 elves
Location: Northwestern coast
Significance: Primary seaport, shipbuilding, maritime trade

The Grey Havens control northwestern sea trade. Master shipwrights build vessels that sail for centuries. Naval power of the Empire.

3. Osgiliath - The Citadel

Population: ~12,000 elves
Location: Eastern border (near Veraheim)

Significance: Military fortress, Dragon Knight headquarters

Fortified city closest to Human Kingdom. Ecthelion (1st Pillar) spends much time here. Dragon Knights train here despite having no dragons.

4. Lothgalen - The Golden Wood

Population: ~18,000 elves
Location: Deep forest, central-west
Significance: Cultural center, arts, music, poetry

Most beautiful of enclaves. Trees golden year-round. Center of elven arts and culture. Poets, musicians, and artists gather here.

5. Ann√∫minas - The Tower of the West

Population: ~10,000 elves
Location: Far western border
Significance: Westernmost outpost, trade hub

Guards western approaches. Trade center for goods from beyond Empire. Ancient tower visible for miles.

6. Imladris - The Last Homely House

Population: ~8,000 elves
Location: Hidden valley, northern forests
Significance: Healing, scholarship, refugees

Sanctuary within the Empire. Welcomes elves in need. Great libraries. Master healers. Pengolodh (2nd Pillar) maintains records here.

7. Edhellond - The Elf-Haven

Population: ~9,000 elves
Location: Southern coast
Significance: Secondary port, fishing, coastal trade

Southern maritime enclave. Fishing fleets provide food. Coastal trade routes. Less formal than Mithlond, more communal.

8. Calembel - The Green Province

Population: ~11,000 elves
Location: Fertile river valley, south-central
Significance: Agriculture, vineyards, food production

Breadbasket of Empire. Produces wine aged for centuries. Fertile lands carefully tended. Tauriel (4th Pillar) oversees economic development.

9. Ethring - The Ford

Population: ~7,000 elves
Location: Major river crossing, central
Significance: Internal trade hub, river commerce

Controls major river crossing. Internal trade routes converge here. Commercial center for inter-enclave trade.

10. Pelargir - The Royal Fleet

Population: ~10,000 elves
Location: Southern coast, near major river
Significance: Naval base, Imperial fleet headquarters

Home of Imperial Navy. Warships built and maintained here. Naval officers trained here. Strategic coastal defense.

Enclave Structure

Common Features:

  • Built over centuries, integrated with ancient forests
  • Architecture emphasizes harmony with nature
  • Local councils govern day-to-day affairs
  • All report to Emperor in Tirion
  • Connected by well-maintained roads
  • Self-sufficient but trade with each other

Travel Between Enclaves:

  • Well-maintained elven roads
  • 3-7 days between most enclaves (walking)
  • Safe travel (Empire patrols roads)
  • Way-stations for rest
  • Courier system for messages

Life in the Enclaves

Daily Life:

  • Peaceful, contemplative existence
  • Work is art (centuries to perfect craft)
  • Strong community bonds
  • Festivals and gatherings common
  • Deep connection to nature and forest

Non-Elf Residents:

  • Small populations in larger enclaves
  • Second-class legal status
  • Restricted rights and employment
  • Tolerated but never equal
  • Drow killed on sight everywhere

The Ten Enclaves: Ancient cities where time moves slowly and tradition endures.
