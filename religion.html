What anyone in Exilium would know

Every race has their own god. Humans worship Solmaren. Elves revere Vaelithar. Dwarves honor Thromdur. Each believes their deity is the true divine power.

They're all worshipping the same god.

This is the great secret of Exilium's cosmos: One divine entity exists. Every racial religion is a different interpretation of the same source. All prayers reach the same destination. The god doesn't correct them.

How Clerics Work

Divine casters draw power from faith, not direct divine contact. A cleric of Solmaren believes in the Sun Father; that belief channels divine energy. The actual god doesn't appear, doesn't speak directly, doesn't correct misconceptions.

  • Domains are disciplines, not different deities
  • Faith powers magic, not divine relationship
  • Different interpretations access the same source differently
  • No god corrects theological disputes

Religious Tolerance (and Intolerance)

Elven Empire: Vaelithar worship dominant. Other faiths tolerated but seen as inferior.

Human Kingdom: Solmaren worship enforced. Other faiths permitted but clergy have less status.

Veraheim: All faiths welcome. Multi-faith temples exist. No state religion.

Broken Isles: Mixed practices. Sea gods, storm gods, various interpretations. Practical faithâ€”pray to whoever helps.

The One God

A single divine entity exists. It has no "true" nameâ€”or rather, all names are equally true. It doesn't prefer one interpretation over another. It doesn't intervene to correct errors.

Why doesn't it speak? Unknown. Theories include:

  • It can't (too vast to communicate directly)
  • It won't (non-interference principle)
  • It doesn't care (mortals are a small part of something larger)
  • It's dead/sleeping (power flows but consciousness doesn't)

False Gods

Beings that claim divinity but aren't:

  • Powerful fiends accepting worship for power
  • Archfey playing god for amusement
  • Ancient sorcerers who achieved near-immortality
  • Entities from beyond the Storm (if it ever breaks)

These beings can grant power (warlock-style), but they're not the One God. Their "clerics" function differentlyâ€”more like warlocks with divine aesthetics.

The Great Storm & Religion

The Great Storm blocks extraplanar contact. This means:

  • No celestials arrive directly
  • No fiends reach through
  • Divine power flows, but divine beings don't manifest
  • This has been true for thousands of years

If the Storm fails: Entities that mortals have been praying to might actually show up. What happens when worshippers meet their "gods" and discover they're not what they expected?

Religious Conflicts as Power Struggles

Theological disputes are actually cultural conflicts. Humans don't fight elves over theologyâ€”they fight over land, power, pride. Religion provides justification.

The irony: Wars fought "in the name of Solmaren" against elves who worship "the wrong god" are actually two groups of people praying to the same deity for victory against each other.

  • The Shared Vision: Clerics of different faiths have the same divine dream. What do they do when they realize they serve one god?
  • The False God: Something claims to be Solmaren incarnate. It grants real power. Is it lying? Does it matter?
  • The Storm Breach: Something from beyond reaches through. It looks like what humans expect Solmaren to look like. But it's not.
  • The Unifying Heresy: A prophet preaches that all gods are one. They're right. Both empires want them dead.
  • The Silent Prayer: A cleric's powers fail. Their faith hasn't changed. What does that mean?
