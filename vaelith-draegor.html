Overview

Vaelith Draegor, known as The Storm or The Sea Lord, is the most powerful pirate in the Broken Islesâ€”and the one who created the confederation itself. A 300-year-old drow who escaped the Empire's genocide of his people, he commands a fleet of 40+ ships and sails aboard Sorrow, an ancient sentient vessel with which he shares a psychic bond.

118 years ago, when the Elven Empire sent a fleet to purge the islands of escaped drow, Vaelith united every captain and crushed them at the Battle of the Broken Straits. The grateful pirates begged him to be their king. He refusedâ€”but the confederation he created that day still operates in his shadow.

He could rule the Broken Isles whenever he wishes. He doesn't want to.

The Ship: Sorrow

Origin

Ancient beyond knowing. Sorrow was already old when Vaelith found it trapped in an underground bayâ€”a ship built by hands long forgotten, for purposes no one remembers. It had waited there in the dark, alone, for centuries. Maybe longer.

Then a broken drow boy washed through the tunnels, half-drowned, having lost everything. They found each other.

The Bond

  • Psychic connectionâ€”no words needed, just knowing
  • Sorrow feels Vaelith's emotions; he feels its... hungers
  • 300 years together, never apart for more than hours
  • The ship is not a tool. It's his partner. His only companion.

Capabilities

  • Faster than any ship has a right to be
  • Can sense vessels before they appear on the horizon
  • Repairs itself slowly (splinters reknit, ropes reweave)
  • Some say it influences the wind around it
  • Vaelith always knows where it is, and it always knows where he is

The Bloodline Oath

Sixty years ago, a captain named Rorik Thenn got drunk in Freehold Harbor and rammed Sorrow while trying to leave harbor. Damaged the hull. An accident.

Vaelith killed Rorik that night. Then his first mate (Rorik's brother). Then sailed to the mainland and spent three months hunting down Rorik's father, his two sisters, his uncle, and his uncle's children.

The Thenn family no longer exists.

No one has touched Sorrow since.

Backstory: The Massacre of Vel'thara

300 Years Ago

Vel'thara was a drow city hidden in deep caverns beneath the mountains. Not large, perhaps three thousand souls, but it was home. They had survived for generations by staying hidden, staying quiet.

The Elven Empire found them anyway.

What Vaelith Lost

  • Ilaena â€” his mother, a weaver, killed defending the children's quarter

The Escape

When the soldiers reached his quarter, Vaelith ran. He found an underground river and jumped, preferring drowning to Imperial blades. The river took him, smashed him through tunnels, and deposited him in a cavern bay lit by phosphorescent moss.

And there, resting in the shallows like it had been waiting... was a ship.

Position in the Broken Isles

Why He Doesn't Rule

"I don't want your throne. I want the sea. Choose your own king. Make your own laws. I will be here if the Empire returns. That is enough."

Vaelith created the confederation but refused to lead it. He doesn't want power, doesn't want responsibility, doesn't want to govern squabbling pirates. He wants to sail, and he wants to kill Empire ships. Everything else is noise.

What He Actually Does

  • Attends The Moot maybe once a year (if something matters)
  • Enforces Harbor Peace by reputation alone
  • Hunts Empire shipping year-round
  • Occasionally appears to "suggest" changes to policy
  • His suggestions are always followed

The Shadow Monarch

Every Pirate King or Queen knows the truth: they rule because Vaelith allows it. If he ever decided to take the throne, no one could stop him. His indifference to power is what makes the current system work.

Queen Graza understands this perfectly. She rules the Isles. He rules the sea. Neither interferes with the other.

Mutual respect. He nodded at her coronationâ€”in the Broken Isles, that's an endorsement. She doesn't bother him with politics; he doesn't undermine her authority. It works.

With the Empire

500,000 gold bounty on his head. Never claimed. Never will be. The hunters become the hunted when they enter his waters.

With Veraheim

Has never approached Veraheim waters. Whether he fears Thorongil or simply doesn't care about the sanctuary city is unknown. The dragon and The Storm have never met.
