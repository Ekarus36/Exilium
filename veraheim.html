üè∞ Veraheim

The Safe Haven - A City Born from Love and Rage

Founded: 80 years ago by Vera Wyrdweaver & Galdor Ruincalen
Population: 3,500 permanent residents
Location: Central mountain pass, 500 feet above sea level
Access: Three magical lifts (North, South, West)
Guardian: Thorongil the dragon, perched on natural peak above
Rulers: Vera Wyrdweaver (The Lady) & Galdor Ruincalen (The Commander)

The City's Core Principle

"All races are equal. Citizenship is earned through contribution, not birth. Merit over bloodline. Always."

What This Means:

  • No Racism: First place in Exilium treating all races equally
  • Merit-Based: Earn your place through work and contribution
  • No Private Property: Housing awarded by contribution tier
  • Retirement System: Lifetime contributors cared for
  • The Hard Truth: No place for those who won't/can't contribute

City Structure - Three Levels

Level 1: Lower Plaza (Ground Level)

Elevation: 500 feet above sea level
Access: Three lift arrival platforms

  • The Grand Plaza: Central gathering space, markets, announcements
  • Mid-Tier Housing: Comfortable apartments for established contributors
  • The Lift Stations: North (Kingdom), South (Harbor), West (Empire)
  • Guard Posts: Security checkpoints at each lift

Level 2: Crafter's District (Middle Level)

Purpose: Workshops, employee housing, economic heart

  • Workshop Quarter: Master craftsmen, guilds, training facilities
  • Employee Housing: Basic housing for new contributors
  • The Market Streets: Shops, taverns, services
  • Guard Barracks: 300 guards housed here

Level 3: The Grand Castle (Summit)

Purpose: Governance, high officials, founders' residence

  • Vera & Galdor's Quarters: Private residence
  • Council Chambers: City governance meetings
  • High Official Housing: Senior administrators, guild leaders
  • Thorongil's Peak: Natural mountain summit above castle

The Contribution System

How to Become a Citizen

Step 1: Entry

Anyone can visit Veraheim during daylight hours via day pass. No contribution status = must leave by sunset.

Step 2: Employment

Find work with established business or guild. Prove your skills and reliability. Most start as apprentices or laborers.

Step 3: Contribution Evaluation

After 6-12 months of consistent work, apply for contribution status. Evaluated on:

  • Skill quality and reliability
  • Community behavior
  • Vouches from employers/neighbors
  • Integration into city life

Step 4: Contributor Status

Granted housing based on tier:

  • Entry Level: Basic shared housing (Level 2)
  • Mid-Tier: Private apartments (Level 1)
  • Senior: Larger quarters (Level 1/3)

Step 5: Master/Retirement

Lifetime contributors earn retirement benefits and permanent housing security.

Economy

How Veraheim Thrives

1. Tunnel Toll Revenue

Only safe east-west passage. Both powers pay toll for commercial traffic. Massive revenue stream.

2. Master Craftsmanship

Diversity + competition = superior products. "Made in Veraheim" commands premium prices throughout Exilium.

  • Dwarf smiths + Gnome enchanters = revolutionary designs
  • Halfling chefs + Human brewers = famous cuisine
  • Elven artisans + Human pragmatism = beautiful functionality

3. Guild Challenges

Every 5 years, any master can challenge guild leadership. Winner gets shop/position. Constant innovation and quality improvement.

Defense

Why Veraheim is Unconquerable

  1. Thorongil the Dragon: Perched on peak, visible for miles. No army can fight an ancient dragon.
  2. 500-Foot Cliffs: Sheer rock walls, unclimbable without magic.
  3. Three Controllable Lifts: Only access points. Vera can disable remotely.
  4. 300 Professional Guards: Commanded by Galdor Ruincalen, trained rigorously.
  5. Defensible Position: Attackers must come up lifts single-file or fly (dragon territory).

The Dark Side

The Floating Docks

Not everyone makes it into Veraheim. Those too poor, too unskilled, or unable to contribute end up on The Floating Docks - lawless boat clusters in the harbor.

~500-800 desperate people live there. Gang violence, poverty, disease. Vera and Galdor don't intervene.

The price of idealism: Can only save 3,500. What about the 501st person?

Daily Life

What It's Like to Live in Veraheim

Morning:

  • Dawn bell rings (work begins)
  • Markets open, craftsmen start work
  • Day workers ascend lifts from Floating Docks
  • Guards change shifts

Day:

  • Workshops busy, markets crowded
  • Tunnel traffic (merchants, travelers)
  • Public audiences (twice weekly - Vera hears petitions)
  • Guard patrols

Evening:

  • Sunset bell (day workers must leave)
  • Taverns fill, families gather
  • Vera visits Thorongil (3-4x per week)
  • Night markets (smaller, quieter)

Night:

  • Reduced activity, residential areas quiet
  • Night guards patrol
  • Thorongil watches from his peak
  • Below, the Floating Docks grow dangerous

Veraheim: Proof that another way is possible. But idealism always has a price.
