What anyone in Exilium would know

Veraheim is a city carved into a split mountainâ€”the only place in Exilium where all races are truly equal.

80 years ago, a human sorceress and an elven dragon rider fell in love, defied their nations, and split a mountain to create a sanctuary. Now 3,500 people live 500 feet above the only safe east-west passage between the Elven Empire and Human Kingdom.

The dragon Thorongil perches above, making the city unconquerable. The tunnel below generates wealth through tolls. And the founders' idealism creates something unprecedented: a merit-based society that judges by contribution, not birth.

Common saying: "In Veraheim, you are what you do, not where you're from."

Getting There

Three magical lifts are the only way up (or down). Each platform holds 20 people or equivalent cargo. Vera's magic powers themâ€”they've never failed in 80 years.

  • East Lift: Faces Kingdom, busiest commercial traffic
  • West Lift: Faces Empire, elven merchants primarily
  • Harbor Lift: Faces sea, connects to small harbor below

The Tunnel Below

Vera's mountain-splitting created a tunnel through the rangeâ€”the only safe east-west passage. Takes 8-12 hours to walk through. Both empires need it desperately. Veraheim charges tolls. This is their primary income.

The Contribution System

Citizenship isn't bought or inheritedâ€”it's earned:

  • Arrive: 30-day provisional status
  • Prove yourself: Demonstrate useful skill
  • Contribute: Regular work for the city's benefit
  • Stay: Permanent residence as long as you contribute

No contributions = no residence. Those who can't or won't contribute must leave by sunset. Most end up on the Floating Docks.

Defense

  • 300 guards under Thaelen's command
  • 500-foot cliffs on all sides
  • Three lift access points easily defended
  • Thorongil - ancient dragon, ultimate deterrent

No army has ever attacked Veraheim. No army ever will while Thorongil lives.

The Lift Vulnerability

Critical secret: The lifts run on Vera's life force.

  • She bound them to herself 80 years ago
  • If she dies, lifts stop working within hours
  • 3,500 people trapped 500 feet up
  • No replacement trained
  • No succession plan

Thaelen knows. He's terrified. They don't talk about it.

The Founding Truth

When Vera split the mountain 80 years ago, she wasn't alone. Both the Empire and Kingdom had sent forces to capture her and Thaelen.

What actually happened: She didn't just split the mountainâ€”she killed dozens of soldiers in the process. Her rage was so great, her power so uncontrolled, that bodies were buried in the rock.

The founders don't talk about this. The official history says they "created a sanctuary." The unofficial truth is they created it on a foundation of blood.

The Floating Docks Problem

Vera sees the Docks every day from her castle. Children drowning. People starving. The contribution system's failures floating below her window.

Her justification: Veraheim can only support so many. Lowering standards would collapse everything. Better some people safe than everyone in ruins.

Her secret: She anonymously funds Mama Kresh's Haven on the Docks. Subsidizes the water barge. Five years ago, gave gold to a halfling familyâ€”but didn't change policy.

She tells herself it's enough. She doesn't believe it.

Thorongil's Perspective

The dragon doesn't just protect Veraheimâ€”he believes in it. He chose this life. Chose exile from dragonkind. Chose to protect these small lives instead of returning to his own kind.

But he's not immortal. Dragons can die. He's 400 years old. He might have centuries leftâ€”or decades. When he goes, Veraheim becomes conquerable.
