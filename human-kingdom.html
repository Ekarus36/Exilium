Fractious, Ambitious, Barely United - Eleven Lords Scheming for Power

Ruler: King Aldric Greymont "The Diplomat King" (52 years old)
Heir: Prince Roderick Greymont (28 years old)
Capital: Thornreich
Government: Feudal monarchy with 11 powerful Lords
Territory: Eastern lands, rolling plains, southeastern coast
Philosophy: Human supremacy, martial strength, might makes right

The Fragile Kingdom

The Central Problem:

The Human Kingdom isn't really a kingdom‚Äîit's eleven nearly autonomous domains held together by economic necessity, a standing royal army, and the King's gold mines. The Lords scheme constantly, alliances shift weekly, and civil war is always one bad decision away.

Why It Holds Together:

  • The King's Gold: Aldric controls richest gold mines, buys loyalty
  • Standing Army: 10,000 soldiers loyal to crown (not Lords)
  • External Threats: Elven Empire keeps Lords unified (barely)
  • Economic Interdependence: Lords need each other's trade
  • Tradition: Monarchy has existed for centuries (inertia)

Why It Could Fall Apart:

  • Lords are nearly as powerful as King
  • Each has own army, own economy, own agenda
  • Constant scheming and backstabbing
  • No Lord fully trusts the King or each other
  • Anyone who conquers Veraheim could claim throne

The King - Aldric Greymont

The Diplomat King (52 years old)

Reputation: Master politician who keeps fractious Lords from killing each other
Strength: Diplomacy, negotiation, reading people
Weakness: Not a warrior, relies on others for military strength

How He Maintains Power:

  • Plays Lords against each other (prevent alliances)
  • Strategic marriages (daughters wed to create alliances)
  • Gold from royal mines (buys loyalty when needed)
  • Standing royal army (10,000 soldiers, not oath-sworn to Lords)
  • Careful balance (never let one Lord get too powerful)

His Challenge:

Every Lord dreams of being King. Any Lord who conquers Veraheim (controls tunnel) could claim enough prestige and wealth to challenge throne. Aldric must keep Lords busy scheming against each other, not against him.

The Heir - Prince Roderick (28)

Personality: Martial, aggressive, impatient
Problem: Wants to be warrior-king, not diplomat-king
Tension: Disagrees with father's cautious approach

Roderick wants to attack Veraheim, claim glory, prove strength. Aldric knows this would be suicide. Father and son clash constantly over Kingdom's direction.

The Eleven Domains

Each Lord rules nearly autonomously within their domain. They collect taxes, maintain armies, enforce laws, and scheme for more power. The King theoretically rules them all, but practically, they do what they want unless he forces the issue.

Human Supremacy - The Ideology

What They Believe:

  • Humans are superior: Adaptable, ambitious, destined to rule
  • Other races are lesser: Tools, servants, threats
  • Strength proves worth: Might makes right
  • Expansion is natural: Humans should dominate

How This Manifests:

Treatment of Non-Humans:

  • Segregated spaces: Separate inns, taverns, districts
  • Second-class legal status: Different laws, harsher penalties
  • Restricted rights: Cannot own property in most domains
  • Limited employment: Menial jobs only, no positions of authority
  • No political representation: Cannot vote or hold office
  • Social discrimination: Open hostility tolerated, encouraged

The Reality: Non-humans in Human Kingdom live as permanent underclass. They can exist, but never thrive. Never equal. Never safe.

Military - Quantity Over Quality

Structure:

  • Royal Army: 10,000 soldiers (loyal to King)
  • Lord Armies: Each Lord maintains own forces (varies, 500-3,000 each)
  • Total: ~25,000-30,000 soldiers across Kingdom
  • Navy: Coastal domains maintain warships

Philosophy:

  • Overwhelming numbers compensate for shorter training
  • Aggressive, offensive tactics preferred
  • Cavalry and heavy infantry focus
  • Less disciplined than elves, more brutal

The Problem:

Lords command their own armies. In theory, they serve the King. In practice, they serve their Lord first. If civil war came, Royal Army (10,000) vs. Combined Lords (15,000-20,000) = King loses.

This is why Aldric must keep Lords divided.

Economy - Competitive & Chaotic

Strengths:

  • Fertile farmland (plains produce abundant crops)
  • Southeastern ports (maritime trade)
  • Gold mines (royal domain, King's wealth)
  • Large population (more workers, more production)
  • Competitive markets (innovation through competition)

Weaknesses:

  • Inconsistent standards (each domain different)
  • Internal tariffs (Lords tax each other's goods)
  • Unstable alliances (trade disrupted by politics)
  • Dependent on Veraheim tunnel (need east-west trade)

The Veraheim Situation:

  • Need tunnel desperately (only safe east-west route)
  • Resent paying tolls to "elf-lover"
  • Lords dream of conquering it (prestige + wealth)
  • King knows attack = suicide (dragon, Vera's power)
  • Economic necessity trumps ideological hatred

Culture & Society

Values:

  • Ambition: Rise through merit and cunning
  • Strength: Martial prowess respected
  • Pragmatism: Do what works
  • Competition: Winners deserve success
  • Glory: Fame and recognition matter

Social Structure:

  • Nobility: Lords, knights, landowners
  • Merchants: Wealthy but not noble
  • Craftsmen: Skilled workers, guilds
  • Peasants: Farmers, laborers, vast majority
  • Non-humans: Permanent underclass

Religion:

Worship Arathor (The Shield, He Who Protects). Martial deity. Strength protects weak, courage defeats evil. Active prayer and martial training as devotion.

Relationship with Veraheim

Official Position:

  • Elves "kidnapped" Vera (human property stolen)
  • Veraheim is illegally occupied human territory
  • Vera under enchantment (can't possibly love elf)
  • Sanctuary undermines human supremacy
  • Would destroy it if they could

Practical Reality:

  • Need tunnel desperately
  • Cannot defeat dragon + Vera
  • Attack = economic disaster
  • Lords would use failure to challenge King
  • Must tolerate what they hate

The Temptation:

Every Lord dreams: Conquer Veraheim ‚Üí Control tunnel ‚Üí Richest domain ‚Üí Challenge for throne ‚Üí Become King

This is why Aldric fears any Lord attempting it.

Current State

Strengths:

  • Large population and military
  • Abundant resources (farms, mines, ports)
  • Competitive economy drives innovation
  • Ambitious people create opportunities

Weaknesses:

  • Constantly on verge of civil war
  • Lords scheme against King and each other
  • No unified vision or long-term planning
  • Dependent on Veraheim's tunnel
  • Supremacy ideology creates enemies
  • Younger, more aggressive heir threatens stability

The Future:

The Kingdom teeters. Aldric keeps Lords divided through diplomacy. But he's 52, Roderick is 28 and aggressive. When Roderick inherits, will he maintain father's careful balance? Or will ambition and ideology drive Kingdom to war it cannot win?

The Human Kingdom: United by supremacy, divided by ambition, held together by one diplomat's skill.
