The King's Balancing Act

King Aldric Greymont maintains power through:

  • Gold mines: Buys loyalty when diplomacy fails
  • Royal tax: 20% of Lords' revenue funds the standing army
  • Standing army: 10,000 soldiers loyal to crown, not Lords
  • Careful balance: Never let one Lord get too powerful
  • Playing Lords against each other: Prevent unified opposition

The Heir Problem: Prince Roderick is martial, aggressive, and impatient. He wants to conquer Veraheim. His father knows that would be suicide. They clash constantly.

Human Supremacy

Like elves, humans believe in their own superiority:

  • Non-humans are second-class citizens
  • Half-breeds treated with suspicion
  • Sorcerers hunted and killed (innate magic = dangerous)
  • Vera's defection seen as betrayal of "her people"

Military

The Lords' Forces (~15,000-20,000 combined): Each Lord maintains own army. Quality varies. Loyal to their Lord, not the King.

The Royal Army (10,000): Professional soldiers, well-trained. Loyal only to the crown. Funded by royal tax. The King's true power.

The Queen's Web

Queen Margarethe Greymont runs the Kingdom's intelligence network. Her ladies-in-waiting are spies. Her charity visits are interrogations. She knows more about the Lords' schemes than her husband does.

What she's discovered:

  • Blackthorne murdered his brother for the title (no proof)
  • Belmont is bankrupt, hiding it with creative accounting
  • Three Lords have secret dealings with Broken Isles pirates
  • Her son Roderick has been manipulated by Eisenwald

Lord Blackthorne's Long Game

Edmund Blackthorne isn't just scheming for more powerâ€”he wants the throne.

His plan:

  1. Weaken rival Lords through manipulation âœ“

  4. When Aldric dies, be positioned as Roderick's chief advisor

What he doesn't know: The Queen suspects him. She's documenting everything.

The Pirate Connections

Three Lords secretly deal with Broken Isles pirates:

  • Blackthorne: Funds specific captains to raid rivals' shipping
  • Belmont: Buys stolen goods at discount, sells as "salvage"
  • Ashfeld: Trades shipping information for cuts of pirate profit

The Succession Crisis

When Aldric dies, the Kingdom will fracture unless:

  • Roderick proves himself quickly
  • The Queen maintains her network
  • No Lord makes a move before Roderick consolidates

The nightmare scenario: Aldric dies before Roderick matures. Multiple Lords declare independence. Civil war.
