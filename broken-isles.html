What anyone sailing these waters would know

The Broken Isles are where the unwanted go to become dangerous.

Twenty volcanic islands, two days' sail from Veraheim, home to pirates, exiles, and the races that don't fit anywhere else. Goblins build ships in Skarath. Hobgoblins forge weapons in Ironhold. Orcs farm and raid from Stormcrag. Bugbears hunt monsters in Mistwood. And everyone pays tribute to whoever sits on the Pirate Throne.

The Moot governsâ€”quarterly assembly where captains and settlements vote. The Pirate Monarch enforces Harbor Peace. But everyone knows the real power: Vaelith "The Storm" Draegor, the drow sea lord whose dreadnought Sorrow could destroy any fleet in the Isles.

Common saying: "In the Isles, you're free to do anythingâ€”if you're strong enough to keep it."

Government: The Moot

Quarterly assembly at Freehold Harbor:

  • Each pirate captain = 1 vote
  • Each settlement = 1 vote
  • Pirate Monarch = tie-breaker
  • Major decisions require 2/3 majority

The Pirate Monarch

Current: Graza Bloodtide (Queen, 3 years)

Powers: Commands largest official fleet, collects tribute, enforces Harbor Peace, settles major disputes, coordinates defense.

Selection: Must command largest fleet (or build alliances). Confirmed by Moot. Can be challenged.

Harbor Peace

The sacred law:

  • Ships traveling to/from Freehold = protected
  • No violence in Freehold harbor
  • Violation = death from ALL captains

Why it works: Makes trade possible. Merchants can safely fence stolen goods. Creates true neutral ground.

Why They Survive

Neither the Empire nor Kingdom can destroy them:

  • Distance (two days' sail)
  • Geography (treacherous waters, many harbors)
  • The Storm (would unite all captains against invaders)
  • Cost (military expedition would be ruinous)

Vaelith Draegor: The True Ruler

Vaelith Draegorâ€”called "The Storm"â€”is a 300-year-old drow who commands the dreadnought Sorrow. He doesn't claim the throne. He doesn't need to.

What he controls:

  • Best ship in the Isles (could defeat any fleet)
  • Network of agents in every settlement
  • Information that could destroy any captain
  • The fear of every soul who sails these waters

His method: He rarely acts. He doesn't need to. When The Storm appears, arguments end. When he speaks, everyone listens.

Graza's Dilemma

Graza Bloodtide is Pirate Queen, but she's not stupid. She knows The Storm tolerates her. If she ever truly challenged him, she'd lose.

Her strategy: Build alliances slowly. Strengthen her fleet. Prove herself indispensable. Wait for The Storm to die (he's mortal, theoretically).

Her fear: What if he never dies? What if being Queen means being his puppet forever?

The Confederation's Fragility

The Isles look united. They're not.

Fault lines:

  • Goblins vs Hobgoblins (mutual contempt, essential trade)
  • Orc clans (alliance is 20 years old, blood feuds are centuries)
  • Freehold vs settlements (captains want freedom; settlements want order)
  • Old pirates vs new (traditions vs innovation)

What holds it together: The Storm's shadow, external threats, economic interdependence, Harbor Peace tradition.

The Mainland Connections

The Isles aren't isolated:

  • Kingdom lords secretly fund pirates to raid rivals
  • Empire merchants secretly buy back stolen goods
  • Saltmere trades with both powers through intermediaries
  • The Storm knows about all of it. He allows it.

What Sleeps in Mistwood

Mistwood hides something ancient. Chief Varka knows. She's sealed the deepest ruins. The Storm has sent agents to investigate; they keep dying.

What's there? Something imprisoned long before the Confederation existed. The bugbears guard it. They don't talk about it.
