üìñ World Overview

The foundation of Exilium - history, themes, and core truths

The Name: Exilium

A world defined by those it casts out. The name reflects:

  • The exile of those rejected by the major powers
  • The self-imposed exile of Vera and Galdor
  • The sanctuary (asylum) they created for the exiled
  • A world where not fitting in means leaving, hiding, or dying

The Two Great Powers

üå≥ The Elven Empire (West)

Capital: Tirion (14 days west of Veraheim)
Ruler: Emperor C√≠rdan Golvenion (1,500+ years on throne)
Government: Absolute monarchy with Four Pillars administration
Population: ~150,000 elves across 10 enclaves

Characteristics:

  • Ancient, stable, controlled
  • Elven supremacy ideology
  • Strategic military (quality over quantity)
  • No death penalty for elves
  • Achievement-based justice system
  • Second-class status for non-elves
  • Kill-on-sight policy for all drow (400 years running)

The Ten Enclaves:

Tirion (capital, 50,000), Mithlond, Osgiliath, Lothgalen, Ann√∫minas, Imladris, Edhellond, Calembel, Ethring, Pelargir

üõ°Ô∏è The Human Kingdom (East)

Capital: Thornreich (14 days east of Veraheim)
Ruler: King Aldric Greymont "The Diplomat King" (52)
Heir: Prince Roderick Greymont (28)
Government: Feudal monarchy with 11 powerful Lords

Characteristics:

  • Fractious, politically unstable
  • Human supremacy ideology
  • Feudal system (nobles vs peasants)
  • Aggressive military (quantity over quality)
  • Lords scheme constantly, alliances shift
  • Non-humans face systematic discrimination: segregated spaces, second-class legal status, restricted rights
  • King barely maintains control through gold mines and standing army

The Eleven Domains:

Each Lord rules nearly autonomously, competing for land, favor, and power. Royal succession passes to eldest son. Daughters married for political alliances.

The Sanctuary: Veraheim

Founded: 80 years ago by Vera Wyrdweaver & Galdor Ruincalen
Location: Central mountain pass, 500 feet above ground
Population: 3,500 permanent residents + temporary visitors
Access: Three magical lifts (only way up/down)
Guardian: Thorongil (Vaethor) the dragon, perched on natural peak above city

Core Principles:

  • All races treated equally
  • Citizenship through contribution (merit-based)
  • No private property - housing awarded by tier
  • Retirement benefits for lifetime contributors
  • No place for the useless - must contribute or leave

Structure:

  • Level 1: Lower Plaza (harbor level, mid-tier housing)
  • Level 2: Crafter's District (employee housing, workshops)
  • Level 3: Grand Castle (high officials, Vera & Galdor)

Economy:

  • Tunnel toll revenue (only safe east-west passage)
  • Master craftsmanship (best goods in the realm)
  • Diversity + competition = superior products
  • Premium prices for "Made in Veraheim"

Defense:

  • 300 guards commanded by Galdor
  • Three lifts (can be disabled instantly)
  • 500ft cliffs (unclimbable)
  • Thorongil the dragon (ultimate deterrent)

The Floating Docks - The Dark Mirror

Location: Open water off Veraheim's harbor
Population: ~500-800 desperate people
Status: Lawless, no official recognition

The Rule: "Off by Sunset"

You can visit Veraheim during daylight hours, but if you cannot afford housing or prove your contribution, you must leave by sunset. The Floating Docks is where they go.

The Reality:

  • Extreme poverty and violence
  • Three criminal gangs prey on residents
  • Children drowning, disease spreading
  • Vera & Galdor don't intervene (resource limits, jurisdiction, no solution)
  • The price of Veraheim's idealism - those who fall through cracks

Major Themes

Merit vs Birth

Veraheim: Contribution and merit determine worth
Empire & Kingdom: Race and bloodline determine worth
Question: Can idealism survive in a hostile world?

Racism & Persecution

Both powers enforce racial hierarchies. Drow Persecution: 400-year genocide by elves. Human Kingdom: systematic discrimination with segregated spaces, restricted rights, and second-class legal status for non-humans. Veraheim proves integration works (but at a cost).

The Cost of Idealism

Veraheim is sanctuary... for 3,500 people. What about the 501st person? The Floating Docks shows the limits. Can't save everyone - who decides who deserves saving?

Forbidden Love

Vera Wyrdweaver and Galdor Ruincalen - human sorcerer + elven dragon rider. Created sanctuary rather than choose sides. Both powers view them as traitors/criminals. Love across boundaries as revolutionary act.

Current Political Situation

The Balance of Power

Stalemate: Elven Empire and Human Kingdom in cold war. Both dependent on Veraheim's tunnel for trade. Neither can claim Veraheim without devastating war. Economic needs override political grievances.

Why No One Attacks Veraheim:

  1. Thorongil: Dragon on the peak = suicide to attack
  2. Defensible: 500ft cliffs, three controllable lifts
  3. Economic: Need the tunnel desperately
  4. Cost: Even if they won, losses would be catastrophic
  5. Other Power: If one attacks, the other might strike them

Tensions:

  • Empire view: Vera enchanted Galdor, stole last dragon rider
  • Kingdom view: Elves kidnapped Vera, Veraheim is stolen territory
  • The Temptation: Any Lord who conquers Veraheim could claim Human throne
  • The Reality: No one can, but dreams persist

The Questions That Define Exilium

Can idealism survive reality?
Veraheim proves integration works. But can only support 3,500 people. The Floating Docks shows the cost.

Is merit enough?
Veraheim judges by contribution. But what about those who can't contribute? Is it fair to demand productivity for survival?

Can wounds heal?
400 years of drow genocide. Centuries of elf-human hostility. Lost dragon bonds. Is reconciliation possible?

What is home?
Exile from homeland. Sanctuary in foreign city. Belonging through contribution, not birth. Where do the unwanted belong?
