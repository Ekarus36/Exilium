80 Years Ago - How Love and Rage Created Veraheim

The Crisis - Mountain Pass Collapsing

80 years ago, the main mountain pass between the Elven Empire and Human Kingdom began collapsing. Lives were at risk on both sides, and the vital trade route was threatened.

The Situation:

  • Natural disaster - mountain instability
  • Hundreds trapped on both sides
  • Pass about to completely collapse
  • Both powers unable to respond quickly enough
  • Time running out for those trapped

The Mission - Eliminate the Threat

Elven Empire's Orders to Galdor:

C√≠rdan sent Galdor Ruincalen, the last dragon rider, with specific orders: Eliminate Vera Wyrdweaver.

Why Target Vera?

  • Most powerful human sorcerer alive
  • Empire viewed her as existential threat
  • If war came, she could devastate elven forces
  • "Remove the threat before it becomes a problem"

Galdor's Mindset:

  • Followed orders without question (400 years of duty)
  • Narrow-minded about humans
  • Believed Empire's propaganda
  • Never questioned if Vera deserved death
  • Prepared to kill her on sight

The Meeting - Choosing Lives Over Orders

At the Collapsing Pass:

Galdor arrived to find Vera already there, using her magic to stabilize the mountain and rescue trapped people.

The Moment:

  • Galdor had clear shot, could have killed her
  • Vera was focused on rescue, vulnerable
  • But she was saving lives - Empire lives too
  • Galdor hesitated
  • Thorongil questioned through their bond: "Is this the enemy?"

The Choice:

Galdor made the first independent decision in 400 years: Help her save lives, kill her after.

Working Together:

  • Vera's magic held the mountain
  • Galdor & Thorongil evacuated trapped people
  • They coordinated perfectly (natural synergy)
  • Saved hundreds of lives together
  • Crisis averted through cooperation

After the Rescue:

Galdor couldn't do it. Couldn't kill someone who just saved his people. The woman he was sent to eliminate had just proven the Empire wrong about humans.

The Connection - Love Across Boundaries

The Days After:

  • They talked (first time really seeing each other)
  • Vera: witty, pragmatic, powerful, kind
  • Galdor: honorable, patient, wise, burdened
  • Both lonely in their own ways
  • Both tired of being weapons for their powers

Thorongil's Role:

The dragon was initially wary of Vera (human intruder), but recognized her character. His acceptance mattered to Galdor.

The Forbidden Love:

  • Human + elf = forbidden by both cultures
  • Sorcerer + dragon rider = natural enemies
  • But they fell in love anyway
  • Knew it would cause problems
  • Chose each other over their peoples

The Hunt - Both Powers Turn Against Them

Elven Empire's Response:

  • Accused Vera of enchanting Galdor
  • "Human witch seduced our last dragon rider"
  • Sent forces to capture him, kill her
  • C√≠rdan personally betrayed and furious

Human Kingdom's Response:

  • Accused elves of kidnapping Vera
  • "Elves stole our greatest sorcerer"
  • Sent forces to rescue her, kill him
  • Refused to believe she chose an elf

The Violence:

  • Both powers hunting them
  • Forced into hiding
  • Skirmishes and deaths
  • Innocents caught in crossfire
  • All because they fell in love

The Rage - Vera Splits the Mountain

The Final Confrontation:

Cornered by forces from both sides, Vera's anger and desperation manifested her full power.

What Triggered It:

  • Innocents dying because of their love
  • Both powers demanding they separate
  • No place in the world for them
  • Galdor wounded protecting her
  • Her rage: "If no place will have us, I'll make one"

The Mountain Split:

Vera unleashed her full power:

  • Split the mountain range itself
  • Created a plateau 500 feet up
  • Carved a tunnel through solid rock
  • Raised sheer cliffs on all sides
  • Made it unconquerable from below

The Magic:

  • Most powerful feat of sorcery in living memory
  • Took days to complete
  • Nearly killed her from exhaustion
  • Changed the geography permanently
  • Both powers watched in terror

The Declaration - A Sanctuary for the Exiled

Vera's Proclamation:

"This is Veraheim - Vera's Home. A sanctuary for those with nowhere else to go. All races welcome. Merit over birth. Come and be safe."

The Core Principles (From Day One):

  • All races treated equally
  • Citizenship through contribution, not birth
  • No racism or supremacy
  • Merit determines worth
  • Protection for the unwanted

The Strategic Position:

  • Tunnel is only safe east-west passage
  • Both powers need trade route
  • Cannot attack without losing access
  • Thorongil on the peak (ultimate deterrent)
  • Vera controls all access (three magical lifts)

The Stalemate:

Both powers wanted to destroy Veraheim, but:

  • Economic dependence on tunnel
  • Dragon on the peak
  • Vera's demonstrated power
  • Defensible position
  • Attacking = economic suicide

The First Years - Building Something New

Who Came:

  • Refugees rejected by both powers
  • Drow fleeing genocide
  • Mixed-race individuals
  • Exiles and outcasts
  • Dreamers who believed in the vision
  • Craftsmen seeking freedom

The Challenges:

  • Building city from nothing
  • Different races learning to coexist
  • Establishing systems that actually worked
  • Defending against raids
  • Proving the vision could survive

Vera & Galdor's Partnership:

  • Vera: City magic, infrastructure, policy
  • Galdor: Military, security, external threats
  • Together: Major decisions, long-term planning
  • Their love: Foundation of everything

80 Years Later - The Legacy

What Veraheim Proved:

  • Integration works: 3,500 people, 12 races living together
  • Merit succeeds: Best goods in realm come from Veraheim
  • Love transcends: Vera & Galdor still together after 80 years
  • Alternative exists: Don't have to choose racism

The Price:

  • Only 3,500 can live there (resource limits)
  • The Floating Docks exist (those who don't make it)
  • Both powers still resent them
  • Constant vigilance required
  • Idealism has limits

The Questions:

  • Can idealism survive reality? (Partially - 3,500 people worth)
  • Can love change the world? (It created sanctuary for thousands)
  • Is merit enough? (For those who make it in, yes)
  • What about those left out? (The Floats - no good answer)

The Meaning of the Founding

"We were rejected by our peoples for loving each other. So we created a place that rejects no one for who they love, what they are, or where they're from. That is Veraheim."

- Vera Wyrdweaver
"I was sent to kill her. Instead, she gave me a reason to live. Eighty years later, that choice remains the best decision I ever made."

- Galdor Ruincalen

The Founding: Love defied empires, rage split mountains, and hope built sanctuary. 80 years later, it endures.
