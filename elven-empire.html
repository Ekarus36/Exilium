What anyone in the world would know

The Elven Empire is ancient, stable, and convinced of its own superiority.

One immortal Emperor has ruled for 1,500 years. Four Pillars administer his will. Ten enclaves house 150,000 elves who live simply by choice while accumulating unimaginable wealth. Their justice system values achievementâ€”the accomplished receive mercy; the unproven receive punishment.

Non-elves are second-class citizens. Drow are killed on sightâ€”a 400-year policy born from the Dragon War that cost elves their dragon bonds forever.

They need Veraheim's tunnel desperately. They hate needing it more.

Common saying: "The Empire does not rush. The Empire endures."

Elven Supremacy

The core ideology:

  • Elves are superior (longer lives = more wisdom)
  • Other races are lesser (shorter lives = limited perspective)
  • Non-elves can live in the Empire but with restrictions
  • Cannot own land, separate courts, harsher penalties
  • Drow policy: Kill on sight. No exceptions. 400 years.

Achievement-Based Justice

Punishment scaled to contribution:

  • High achievers (scholars, artists): Lightest sentences
  • Low achievers (laborers, young): Harsh sentences
  • Non-elves: Harshest, including execution
  • No death penalty for elves (centuries of wisdom too valuable)

Economy: Mutual Provision

Enclaves don't pay gold taxesâ€”they share goods. Each produces what it's suited for; Faendril's office distributes based on need. Currency exists only for external trade.

The Veraheim Problem: The Empire desperately needs the tunnel but must pay tolls to traitors. Faendril manages this humiliation daily.

The Dragon War Truth

The official history: Drow tortured dragons, breaking sacred bonds. The Empire righteously exterminated them.

The hidden truth (known only to Valandor, Velindor Shadowmere, and the deepest archives):

  • The war began over territory, not dragons
  • Both sides committed atrocities
  • The dragon-torture was real but was retaliation, not unprovoked
  • Elves struck first
  • The genocide was Valandor's rage, not justice

Why it matters: If this became public, the Empire's moral foundation crumbles.

Velindor Shadowmere - Master of Whispers

The Empire's spymaster knows almost everything. He's served four Emperors. He's killed for the Empire more times than he can count.

What haunts him: He knows the Dragon War truth. He's buried evidence. He's silenced witnesses. He did it for the Empire. He'd do it again.

His network: Agents in Veraheim (discovered and tolerated), agents in the Human Kingdom (several lords compromised), agents in the Broken Isles (limited success).

The Four Pillars' Secrets

  • Caerwyn: Wants war with Veraheim. Has drawn up invasion plans. Knows they'd fail but updates them anyway.
  • Thaelorin: The Living Archive contains records that would destroy the Empire. He's read them. He's sealed them.
  • Isileth: Has been in secret contact with Vera Wyrdweaver for 60 years. They discuss philosophy, not politics. Valandor doesn't know.
  • Faendril: Has secretly invested Empire gold in Veraheim-based businesses. If exposed, it would be treason.

Thaelen's Defection: The Real Story

Thaelen didn't betray the Empire for love. He betrayed it because he discovered what the Empire did to start the Dragon War.

His choice: Stay and live a lie, or leave with the woman he loved. He chose Veraâ€”and took his dragon, the last bonded dragon in the Empire.
