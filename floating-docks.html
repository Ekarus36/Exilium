What anyone approaching Veraheim would know

Anchored in the harbor below Veraheim floats a collection of lashed-together boats, rafts, and derelict vesselsâ€”home to those who couldn't make it into the sanctuary above.

The Floating Docks represent the price of Veraheim's idealism. The contribution system works because failures are expelled. The expelled have nowhere to go. So they stay, floating in the shadow of the city that rejected them.

No law. No protection. Three gangs fight for territory. Children learn to swim before they walk. People drown in storms. Vera watches from her castle every single day.

Veraheim's rule: Those who cannot contribute must leave by sunset. The Docks are where they go.

Physical Structure

Boats lashed together with rope and chain. Platforms built on barrels. Walkways that shift with waves. Lean-tos and tents. Everything rots. Everything sinks eventually.

Sections:

  • The Core: Oldest, most stable platforms. Gang territories.
  • The Ring: Newer additions around the edges. Less stable.
  • The Fringes: Single boats, isolated families. Dangerous.

Vera's Secret Support

Vera anonymously funds three things on the Docks:

  1. Mama Kresh's Haven: Monthly gold through intermediaries
  2. The Water Barge: Subsidized to never run out
  3. Emergency Medicine: A healer who "volunteers"

She's never told anyone except Thorongil. She doesn't change policy. She just... makes it slightly less terrible. She hates that this is all she does. She does it anyway.

The Halfling Girl

Five years ago, an 8-year-old halfling girl asked Vera why her family couldn't live in the city. They'd been expelled when her father got sick and couldn't work.

Vera explained the contribution system. The girl said her baby brother got sick. They spent everything on medicine. Now they had nothing.

Vera gave them goldâ€”enough for two months. Anonymously. Then went back to her castle and cried. She told only Thorongil.

She didn't change the policy.

This haunts her more than anything else she's done.

The Docks' True Purpose

The Docks serve a function Veraheim doesn't acknowledge: they're a warning.

Every citizen who thinks about slacking, about coasting, about not contributingâ€”they look down and see the Docks. They see what happens to those who fail. The fear keeps them working.

Vera knows this. She uses it. She hates that she uses it.

Rising Tension

The Docks population has grown in recent years. More refugees from Kingdom persecution. More failed applicants as Veraheim's standards rise.

Someone is organizing. Quietly. Carefully. They're not planning violenceâ€”yet. They're planning to present a united front. Demand recognition. Demand change.

When that day comes, Vera will have to choose: expand Veraheim beyond its capacity, or refuse people she's already watching suffer.
