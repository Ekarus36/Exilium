‚öì The Floating Docks

The Dark Mirror of Veraheim - Where Idealism Meets Its Limits

Population: ~500-800 people (fluctuates constantly)
Location: Anchored in open water off Veraheim's harbor
Status: Lawless, no official recognition
Nickname: "The Floats," "The Drowned Quarter"

Overview - The Cruel Truth

The Floating Docks are the grim reality that exists in the shadow of Veraheim's idealistic sanctuary. Where Veraheim represents hope through contribution and merit, the Floats represent what happens to those who cannot meet that standard‚Äîor cannot afford to try.

"You can visit Veraheim during daylight hours, but if you cannot afford housing or prove your contribution, you must leave by sunset. The Floating Docks is where they go."

The Rule: "Off by Sunset"

Who Lives Here

The Desperate:

  • People who come to Veraheim but cannot afford entry
  • Visitors not allowed to stay after sunset (non-contributors)
  • Day laborers who earn enough to survive but not enough for city housing
  • Refugees too poor to qualify for citizenship
  • Those who failed the evaluation period
  • Families saving money to afford city entry

The Unwanted:

  • Criminals fleeing justice from Empire or Kingdom (no extradition here)
  • Outcasts even Veraheim won't accept
  • Black market traders avoiding city oversight
  • Those expelled from Veraheim for minor infractions

The Hopeful:

  • Young people trying to prove themselves worthy
  • Craftsmen building reputation to win shop challenges
  • Anyone still dreaming of earning a place above

Structure of the Floats

Each "float" is an independent artificial island made of boats, barges, rafts, and debris lashed together with rope, chain, and desperation. They drift in a loose cluster, anchored in open water just beyond Veraheim's jurisdiction.

Notable Establishments

The Rusty Anchor (Largest Tavern Float)

Owner: Marin Saltwhisker (Human, male, 52)
Former sailor who lost his ship in a storm. Weathered, one eye scarred shut. Tries desperately to stay neutral between gangs.

Structure: 20+ boats lashed together
Capacity: ~80 people at peak times

Services:

  • Watered-down ale (1 copper)
  • Fish stew (2 copper)
  • Hammock space (1 copper per night)
  • Information brokering (if you know who to ask)

Character: Loud, crowded, dangerous after dark. Neutral ground during day. Pays protection to whoever asks.

Mama Kresh's Haven

Owner: Kreshna "Mama Kresh" Ironheart (Half-Orc, female, 45)
Fierce protector of her customers. Lost her family in Human Kingdom persecution. Tolerates no violence in her establishment.

Structure: 8 boats, cozy and cramped
Capacity: ~30 people

Services:

  • Actual food (not just stew) - 3 copper
  • Clean beds (not hammocks) - 2 copper per night
  • Safety (gangs don't mess with Mama)
  • Motherly advice (free)
  • Children get free meals

Character: Warmest place on the Floats. Families prioritized. Only place where you're truly safe at night.

Secret: Vera anonymously funds Mama Kresh's Haven. Mama doesn't know who her benefactor is.

The Three Gangs

Three criminal organizations prey on Float residents, each controlling different territories and rackets.

1. The Tide Reapers

Leader: "Captain" Darius Blackwater (Human, male, 38)
Territory: Southern floats (closest to open sea)
Specialty: Smuggling and piracy

Operations:

  • Control smuggling routes to/from Veraheim
  • Protection racket (pay or boats "accidentally" sink)
  • Black market goods (stolen, contraband, illegal)
  • Occasional piracy on merchant vessels

Character:

Most organized and professional. Prefer intimidation over violence (bad for business). Will negotiate if you have leverage. Respect strength and cunning.

2. The Barnacle Boys

Leader: "Rusty" Kelvin Sharptooth (Half-Elf, male, 29)
Territory: Eastern floats (near Veraheim harbor)
Specialty: Petty crime and exploitation

Operations:

  • Pickpocketing and mugging
  • Loan sharking (brutal collection methods)
  • Forced labor (debt slavery)
  • Selling fake Veraheim work permits

Character:

Youngest and most violent gang. Desperate to prove themselves. Unpredictable and cruel. Prey on the weakest residents. Most dangerous to common people.

3. The Sinkers

Leader: Gresha "The Drowner" Coalgrim (Dwarf, female, 67)
Territory: Western floats (furthest from Veraheim)
Specialty: Assassinations and disposal

Operations:

  • Contract killings (cheap rates)
  • Body disposal (weighted, sunk, disappeared)
  • Kidnapping for ransom
  • Sabotage and arson

Character:

Smallest but most feared. Methodical and efficient. Silent operations (violence without witnesses). Gresha is calm, professional, terrifying. "Bodies don't float if you weight them right."

Daily Life on the Floats

Morning (Dawn):

  • Water barge arrives (1 copper per bucket)
  • Day workers row to Veraheim for work
  • Fishermen set nets (food for the day)
  • Children scavenge debris for salvage
  • Gangs still asleep (partied through night)

Day:

  • Relatively safe (gangs keep violence down during day)
  • Trading and bartering between floats
  • Repairs to boats and structures
  • Hopeful check Veraheim job boards
  • Families gather at Mama Kresh's

Evening (Sunset):

  • The bell rings from Veraheim (day workers must leave)
  • Boats return carrying exhausted laborers
  • Taverns fill up quickly
  • Gangs emerge for "business"
  • Families lock themselves in boats

Night:

  • Dangerous. Stay inside or at Mama Kresh's.
  • Gang violence, muggings, disappearances
  • Tide Reapers collect protection money
  • Barnacle Boys prowl for victims
  • The Sinkers conduct "jobs"
  • Morning often brings floating bodies

Veraheim's Position

Why Vera and Galdor Don't Intervene

1. Jurisdiction:

The Floats are in international waters, technically outside Veraheim's authority. Any military action would be seen as aggression by both major powers.

2. Resource Limits:

Veraheim has 300 guards for 3,500 residents. Cannot spare forces to police the Floats.

3. No Solution:

Break up the gangs, more would form. Bring everyone into city, population becomes unsustainable. Build housing on Floats, it becomes Veraheim-2 (same problems).

4. Overflow Valve:

The Floats prevent worse alternatives. Without it, desperate people would storm Veraheim, camp outside walls, create bigger crisis.

5. The Guilt:

Vera sees the Floats from her castle every day. Knows children are drowning. This eats at her. But she won't change course.

What Veraheim Does Do:

  • Allows day work passes (Float residents can work in city during daylight)
  • Water barge delivers clean water daily (subsidized by Veraheim)
  • Guards protect anyone who reaches the lifts (once you touch them, you're safe)
  • Vera secretly funds Mama Kresh's Haven
  • Veraheim will help anyone who earns citizenship (path exists, just difficult)

The Moral Question

"Where do we draw the line? If we help the Floats, they'll grow. More will come. Do we expand to 4,000? 5,000? 10,000? At what point does Veraheim collapse trying to save everyone?"

- Vera Wyrdweaver

The Floating Docks represent the fundamental tension at the heart of Exilium:

  • Can idealism survive reality?
  • Is merit-based citizenship just? Or cruel?
  • Who decides who deserves saving?
  • Is Vera right to draw the line at 3,500?
  • Or is she a hypocrite who could do more?

There are no easy answers.

Adventure Hooks

  • Gang war threatens to spill into Veraheim
  • Mama Kresh asks party to escort refugee family
  • Mysterious benefactor hiring to break gang power
  • Child from Floats stows away on party's boat
  • Tide Reapers offer lucrative smuggling job
  • Discover Vera's secret funding of Haven
  • Help Float residents organize and improve conditions
  • Investigate disappearances (Sinkers at work)

The Floating Docks: Where idealism meets its limits, and the question "who deserves saving?" has no good answer.
